# Concept image targets – canonical reference

## Status

Active. Maps each image in the top-level `concept-images/` folder to the room element or arrangement it is the visual target for. Decisions recorded 6 June 2026 in conversation with Riaan. When building or reviewing any of these elements, open the named image first – the image is the brief.

Image 11 was removed from the folder before review; numbering keeps the gap.

## Whole-room targets

- **Image 04** – the target look for the room overall in experience mode: deep darkness, green points overhead, a person on a dark cube stool, one warm glowing panel. When judging any render, compare against this frame.
- **Image 09** – the target look for the **fireflies nesting** arrangement specifically: dark rounded pebble-like ceiling forms with green points set into their undersides, the warm glowing wall on the left, dark navy curtain on the right, dark cube stools scattered below. Rebuild the nesting arrangement to match this image.
- **Image 10** – the target look for the **fireflies within reach** arrangement: flat dark discs overhead with long thin branches hanging down from them, green points among the discs, warm light pools washing the walls, low bench enclosures below.
- **Image 13** – the target look for the **fireflies flock** arrangement: dense vertical strings of green points draping every wall like rain, large dark disc silhouettes floating against a glowing ceiling field, a warm doorway at the centre.

## Ceiling

- The ceiling vocabulary is **flat rounded discs** (seen in images 07, 09, 10, 13). The sculptural timber objects in images 01 and 02 are secondary inspiration only.
- Green points stay over the forest area only. They do NOT extend over the pathway – the few corridor dots visible in images 05 and 14 are incidental.

## Loofah wall

Two looks, kept as switchable options to compare:

- **Neat grid** (image 06) – an ordered grid of warm backlit glowing squares.
- **Organic fibrous** (image 07) – wild loofah texture criss-crossed with dark sticks, lit from behind.
- **Image 08** is the close-up texture target for either look: when the camera gets near, the individual fibres should read as fine dark lines against the amber glow.
- **Image 12** adds three details that are all in: a bright light strip along the floor at the base of the wall, a layer of low mist drifting at floor level, and the storytelling beat of a visitor close enough to touch the wall.

## Pathway

Two looks, kept as switchable options to compare:

- **Dark walls + ceiling downlights** (image 05) – near-black corridor, warm strips along both floor edges, small warm pools from above.
- **Warm timber + wall lamps** (image 14) – wood-panelled corridor, small glowing wall lamps down one side, amber strips along both floor edges.

## Seating

The three seating options are replaced by:

1. **Solid wooden cubes** (image 03) – solid block stools, loosely clustered in social groups. Image 03 gives both the cube design and the informal clustering pattern. In the dark room frames (images 04, 09, 15) the cubes read dark.
2. **Open-frame wood stools** (image 12) – lighter timber stools built from an open frame.
3. **Benches** (image 15) – low boxy benches.

**Image 15 also redesigns the seat lighting for every arrangement:** each seat sits under its own soft white downbeam, replacing the previous three shared zone spotlights. The warm glowing panel in image 15 is the focal point the seats face.
