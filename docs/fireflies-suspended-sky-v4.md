# Fireflies-suspended-sky – v4

## What the room is

- Flat plywood panels suspended at varying heights and slight tilts.
- Matte near-black, green LED pinpoints scattered across each panel face.
- Organic arrangement, not a grid; gaps between panels; visible cables.
- Panel details estimated for now.

## How visitors move

- Entry at entrance-wall/back-wall corner.
- Along back-wall through the tunnel.
- Turn along window-wall through the tunnel.
- Exit at column/front-wall gap into the forest.
- One-way.

## Tunnel (5–7 min)

- Shoes off; foam mat and fake grass.
- 10 flashlights first come first served; 20 of 30 walk in without one.
- Flashlight body has small visible glow.
- Wall panels with firefly facts, backlit, photographable; phones in tunnel only.
- Final panel: soft hint about settling the beam.

---

## Lighting option A: sundown

- Hidden LED strips behind baffles along all four walls; source never visible.
- Entry: warm amber – room feels like last light before dark.
- Slow continuous fade through orange-red to deep blue-black across the session.
- Wall colour is the room's clock – visitors feel time passing without being told.
- Green firefly LEDs read warm and soft against amber at entry; cold and precise against blue-black by stage 3.
- Panels catch amber at entry; by deep night invisible except for the green points.
- By stage 3 wall LEDs are at deep blue-black or off – room at its darkest as synchrony peaks.

## Lighting option B: breath-paced wall light

- Hidden LED strips behind baffles along all four walls; source never visible.
- Colour: very dim warm amber – constant throughout, no colour transition ever.
- Pulses at lung rate: ~4 seconds brightening, ~6 seconds dimming.
- Slow enough that visitors may not immediately notice it is moving.
- Completely independent of the firefly system – the two rhythms never sync.
- The tension between wall breath and firefly flicker is the atmospheric quality of this variant.
- Green firefly LEDs sit inside the warm pulse – cool and precise against soft and warm.

---

## Stage 1: flicker

- Room already alive; fireflies waking and fading across panels on their own.
- No visitor input; group watches.
- 1–3 minutes.

## Stage 2: interactive

- Holders sweep beams at panels; firefly pool wakes wherever beam lands, ~2–3m wide.
- Move beam, pool moves; beam away, fades back.
- Holding still builds a denser pool over time.
- 10 moving pools; watchers cluster around holders in groups of 2–3.
- Pattern legible within 2–3 minutes; realisation spreads quietly.
- 6–8 minutes.

## Stage 3: programmed movement

- ~70% of flashlights down or still triggers synchrony build.
- All panels pulse in unison over 2–3 minute build.
- Peak: ~30 seconds; most coherent moment, not the brightest.
- Decohere over ~1 minute; thunder; room dims; group exits.
