# Phase 4: marketing and promotion

Priority: medium.

## Two separate narratives

### Promotional narrative (outward-facing)

For marketing, social media, and press. Frame the exhibition as:

A world-class immersive art exhibition in the mountains of Alishan, where technology meets indigenous culture and ecological awareness. A collaboration between Nanghia and its creative team, bringing art + science + tech to one of Taiwan's most biodiverse firefly habitats. Not a light show - an art exhibition that uses living light to explore the connection between humans and nature.

Emphasize: the unique mountain location, the teamLab-inspired immersive experience, the Tsou community connection through Nanghia, and the ecological message.

### Exhibition narrative (inward-facing)

The story visitors experience inside the space. This appears on the entrance statement wall, audio guides, and printed materials. Use the storyline from phase 3: fireflies as a bridge between humans and nature, bioluminescence as a parallel to brain synapses, the Buddhist reflection concept, positive reinforcement.

## SEO and geo targeting

The website must include:

- Meta descriptions and page titles optimized for: "Alishan exhibition," "immersive art Taiwan," "firefly experience Alishan," "阿里山展覽," "螢火蟲體驗," and similar terms in both English and Chinese.
- Geo-targeted content referencing nearby landmarks: 德恩亞納 village ("little Switzerland"), Alishan National Scenic Area, and the Alishan forest railway.
- Schema markup for event/exhibition type.
- The tourist framing: most visitors are already traveling to the Alishan area, so this exhibition is an easy add-on to an existing trip. The village has a B&B scene that is growing rapidly.

## Tourism platform copy

Create a section with pre-written copy formatted for submission to:

- Alishan National Scenic Area tourism website.
- Local tourism bureau listings.
- Travel platforms: KKday, Klook, Trip.com.
- Google Maps / Google Business listing.

Each listing needs: a short description (under 150 characters), a medium description (under 300 characters), and a full description (under 600 characters). All in both English and Chinese.
