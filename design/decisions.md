# Decisions — what's locked

The single list of settled decisions and fixed facts. Newest dated entry always
wins. If something here is being reopened, move it to [open.md](open.md); don't
leave it half-settled in both places.

A "locked" item is closed. Don't offer alternatives, don't re-tune it, build to
it. Dates are when the decision was made (where known).

## The project, in one line

An immersive firefly exhibition room in Alishan, Taiwan. Visitors walk a dark,
gallery-like space and move through a sunset-into-night cycle lit by fireflies,
arriving at a quiet realisation: the fireflies are a reflection of themselves.
Wonder → connection → care. The web tool is a private preview for planning the
real room — it is not the product. The room is.

## The room (physical)

- **Shape and size — locked.** Rectangular. Front-wall and back-wall run one way,
  window-wall and entrance-wall the other; the working ceiling sits below the
  original structural ceiling after the beams. *(Exact metres live in the
  measurements file — see [room.md](room.md). Never re-type them here.)*
- **Visitor entrance — locked.** A single full-height opening, between the column
  and the front-wall.
- **Element names — locked.** Every part of the room has one hyphenated name:
  front-wall, back-wall, window-wall, entrance-wall, pathway, pathway-partition,
  entrance-wall-partition, column, forest, exhibition-area, hvac-plenum,
  silver-service-door. Always say which partition you mean — never just
  "partition." The walking strip is always "pathway," never "corridor" or
  "walking strip." Old names (corridor, Segment 1/2/3, EntryPathway) are banned.
- **Window-wall layout — locked.** Walking from the front-wall corner toward the
  back-wall corner: a stretch of plain wall with the small window cut into it, then
  the silver service door, then a short plain stretch, then the main glass
  partition running flush to the back-wall corner. *(Exact widths in
  [room.md](room.md).)*
- **The column is existing concrete — locked.** It's a structural part of the
  building, sitting just outside the room in the corridor, not part of the new
  build. The two partitions, by contrast, are new builds.
- **The two partitions are storage cabinets — locked 13 June 2026.** Roughly half
  a metre thick, with internal storage (not thin plywood). They also carry
  fireflies (see below).
- **"Aircon" and "HVAC" are two different things — locked vocabulary.** "Aircon
  units" = the air-conditioning heads on the back-wall. "HVAC" = the plenum box on
  the window-wall. Never treat them as the same fixture; use Riaan's word back.

## Fireflies — locked

- **Green, never yellow.** Matches the real Taiwan firefly and the brand palette.
  Yellow reads as cartoon and breaks the tone.
- **They are lights behind fabric, not floating dots.** Micro-LEDs sit behind
  cloth-covered surfaces and glow through as soft, diffused points. Their
  animation is fading and pulsing in place, not flying around the room.
- **They glow from the ceiling forms and the partition cabinets** (locked 13 June
  2026). This replaced the earlier "ceiling + entrance-wall + window-wall"
  placement and the earlier "LEDs only on the ceiling" note — the entrance-wall
  and window-wall no longer carry fireflies. No other surface carries fireflies
  either — not the front-wall (loofah), the back-wall, the entrance-wall, or the
  window-wall. Ceiling forms and partition cabinets only.
- **Each firefly point is true-to-life: 3 mm** (locked 13 June 2026). The preview
  shows them at their real size so it matches what gets built — never enlarged for
  on-screen visibility. If the room reads as too sparse, the answer is more or
  brighter fireflies, never bigger dots.

## Lighting and the sunset — locked 6 June 2026

- **All light comes from real fixtures.** The sunset-into-night cycle (golden hour
  → twilight → blue hour → darkness) is produced by physical lights that could
  actually be built: light strips on the walls and partitions, lights inside the
  cabinets, and colour-changing spotlights. **Never** a scene-wide invisible
  colour wash — Riaan rejected that outright ("how would that even work in a real
  room?").
- **The wall strips are the sunset's horizon.** Their colour and brightness follow
  the timeline through researched real-sunset steps (gold → fiery orange → blush
  pink → ember → out). This replaced the earlier "freeze the orange and wait for
  client review" decision.
- Sunset colours come from real sunset research, not invented values.

## Wayfinding — locked

- **One system only:** always-on warm light strips along the pathway edges, plus
  three matte-white painted arrows. No light fixtures for the arrows (they're
  paint), and no switcher between wayfinding options.
- The pathway always keeps the curtain on the window-wall side; never clad the
  side where the real glass partition is.

## The pathway — locked 13 June 2026

- **A brightness journey, not a cladding choice.** It starts bright at the
  entrance — gallery-style spotlights — so visitors can read information mounted
  on the partition there. It then darkens progressively as they walk, until total
  darkness at the threshold into the forest.
- **Floor-level track lights** run along the pathway.
- **No wood panelling.** The pathway is a dark, near-black corridor (the old
  "timber / wood-panelled" look was removed completely — it was never a real
  option, just a previous session mislabelling a reference image).
- *(The forest has its own track lights and spotlights — that's separate from the
  pathway.)*

## Mist — locked 13 June 2026

- **Low ground mist that rises up from the floor** — like forest-floor fog at
  dusk. **Never** haze coming down from the ceiling.
- *(The physical install still needs venue sign-off — smoke detectors, airflow —
  but the design intent is settled: floor-rising mist only.)*

## The visitor experience — locked

- **Cohorts of up to 30, together, for 15–20 minutes.** People are let in in
  small batches of about five for door flow, but once inside, everyone moves
  through the cycle as one synchronised group — all reach golden hour, the bloom,
  and the mirror beat at the same moment. Then all 30 leave and the next group
  begins. No rolling overlap, no one arriving mid-cycle.
- **The arc is personal and collective at once.** Even though all 30 share each
  beat, the final realisation lands privately. The crowd amplifies the personal
  feeling, it doesn't replace it.

## The web tool — locked

- **It's one page.** The 3D room with a picker for trying lighting and firefly
  options. The old separate addresses just redirect to it. No marketing site, no
  second sandbox.
- **It's private.** The 3D preview is only for Riaan and Corbett to plan and test
  — not public-facing. Don't optimise it for public users or mobile visitors.
- **Outside the room walls reads white by default** (for reviewing the room
  shape), and only goes near-black inside the experience-mode preview.

## Project-wide rules — locked

- **No attribution, ever.** No designer, artist, studio, or specific-artwork
  names anywhere — not in the room, the tool, the docs, the file names, or commit
  messages. The form carries the reference.
- **No Tsou / sacred-mountain cultural narrative.** Indigenous context is
  background research only, not a concept hook.
- **Cover, don't celebrate.** Existing infrastructure (pipes, ducts, doors, the
  old ceiling) gets hidden or transformed, never featured.
