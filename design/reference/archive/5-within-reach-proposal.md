# Within-reach proposal – canonical reference

## Status

Active. One of two design options for the firefly room. The other is `folded-sky` (see doc 4). Both share the same room and most elements. The differentiator: this proposal adds real branches with LEDs hanging below the ceiling, close enough to almost touch.

## Room composition (this proposal)

References room spec (doc 1), ceiling (doc 2), loofah wall (doc 3) for all dimensional and material values. Specific to this proposal:

- Sculptural suspended ceiling (per doc 2).
- Real branches (bamboo or locally available hardwood) wired horizontally BELOW the ceiling at varying heights.
- Green LEDs on both ceiling forms AND branches.
- 1,760 LEDs total across 110 modules, split between ceiling and branches. Exact ratio TBD once ceiling form count and branch count are locked.
- Loofah wall on `front-wall` (per doc 3).
- Cabinet partitions (per doc 1).
- Interlocking foam mat floor throughout (per doc 1). Shoes on.

## Branches

### Material

- Bamboo (most likely – locally available in Taiwan).
- OR locally available hardwood.

### Arrangement

- Sparse and gestural reads as intentional.
- Crowded reads as decor.
- Requires aesthetic lead on install day.

### Constraints

- Minimum clearance over walkable areas: 2.2m. Verify install-day.
- Branch durability: branches dry, drop bark, shift colour over time. Acceptable for short runs. If installation extends beyond 2–3 weeks, plan replacement cycles.

### LED power method (UNDECIDED – blocking)

Two options, decision required BEFORE branch rigging begins:

- **Wired LEDs**: clean visually with rough-barked branches and thin wire, but requires cable route per branch to the ceiling and down to power distribution. Complex with organic arrangement.
- **Battery LEDs**: no cable management problem, but each unit needs battery swap between sessions. Significant operational load if sessions run several times a day.

The choice determines branch preparation AND whether ceiling needs cable routing built into anchor points.

### Rigging

- Anchor points or rail system on ceiling for branch suspension.
- Anchor count, load rating, adjustability: TBD with carpenter.
- A rigid grid of anchor points gives more flexibility on install day.
- A fixed set of points constrains arrangement options.

## Forest experience – three stages

### Stage 1: flicker

- Duration: 1–3 minutes.
- Fireflies wake and fade across BOTH branches and ceiling forms.
- Vertical layering visible from group entry – fireflies overhead, at head height, and lower.
- No visitor input.

### Stage 2: interactive

- Duration: 6–8 minutes.
- Visitors sweep beams at branches AND ceiling forms.
- Beam contact (on branch or ceiling form): pool of fireflies wakes around it.
- Holders at different heights work in different vertical zones simultaneously.
- Low branches: most intimate response – fireflies almost within reach.
- 10 moving pools through the branch + ceiling system.

### Stage 3: programmed movement

- Duration: 2–3 minutes.
- Trigger: approximately 70% of flashlights pointed down or held still.
- Fireflies across ALL branches and ALL ceiling forms pulse in unison.
- Synchrony reads three-dimensionally – whole room breathes at once.
- Programmed movement patterns possible across both surfaces.
- Peak: ~30 seconds.
- Decoherence + thunder + dim + exit.
