# The experience

What happens to a visitor, start to finish. The locked parts (the cohort format,
the emotional arc, lighting-from-fixtures) are in [decisions.md](decisions.md);
the choices still open (the storyline, the firefly arrangement) are in
[open.md](open.md). This page ties it together.

## The two stories — keep them separate

- **Outer story (promotional):** the story *of* the project — what it is, why it
  exists, why you'd visit. Marketing language. *(There is no public website right
  now; this is the framing, not a live surface.)*
- **Inner story (the experience):** what happens *inside* the room — the journey
  visitors walk through and inhabit. No text panels, no explanation. The room
  tells it through light, darkness, and rhythm.

Both must land for a non-art audience: families, tourists, casual travellers
already visiting Alishan. The inner story has to work without any art-world
knowledge.

## The emotional arc

**Wonder → connection → care.** Visitors feel awe through the fireflies and the
atmosphere, then arrive at a quiet realisation: the fireflies are not separate
from them — they're a reflection of them. They leave seeing nature differently,
wanting to protect it because it's part of them. Positive, not guilt or doom.

The underlying idea: a firefly's glow and the signals in our own minds are the
same kind of living light. Everything you see is an extension of yourself.

## The shape of a visit

- A group of up to 30 people goes through together, over **15–20 minutes**.
- They're let in in small batches of about five (for flow through the doors), but
  once inside they move through the cycle **as one synchronised group** — everyone
  hits golden hour, the bloom, and the final beat at the same moment.
- Then all 30 leave and the next group starts. No rolling overlap.
- It's personal and collective at once: the realisation lands privately, but the
  crowd around you amplifies the feeling.

## The lighting cycle

A real sunset into night — **golden hour → twilight → blue hour → darkness** —
driven entirely by physical fixtures in the room (wall and partition light
strips, cabinet lights, colour-changing spotlights), never an invisible wash. The
colours come from real sunset research. This cycle is the backbone of the
experience; the firefly behaviour rides on top of it.

## The three stages inside the room

Across the proposals, the in-room journey runs in three stages:

1. **Flicker / awakening** (short) — the fireflies wake, soft and scattered, as
   the light falls.
2. **Interactive** (the long middle) — visitors can affect the fireflies (the
   exact mechanic depends on the storyline chosen).
3. **Programmed climax** (short) — a synchronised peak (the bloom), then a calm
   decoherence and the mirror beat, then return.

## The choices still being made

These are not decided — full detail in [open.md](open.md):

- **The storyline** — one of three: *the room remembers*, *I called one*, or *the
  forest doesn't need you*. Each gives stage 2 a different interactive mechanic.
- **The firefly arrangement** — one of six: suspended-sky, within-reach, flock,
  grove, lanterns, or nesting. These change the furniture and the visitor's
  spatial relationship to the fireflies above, never the LED positions.
- **The big interactive unknown** — whether a flashlight can actually summon or
  scatter the fireflies has never been prototyped. It gates the interactive
  storylines.

## The web preview's role

The 3D page is where Riaan and Corbett compare these options before building —
trying lighting modes and firefly arrangements side by side. It is a private
planning tool, not the experience itself. The experience is the real room.
