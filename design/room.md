# The room

A plain-language picture of the physical space. The big decisions about it are in
[decisions.md](decisions.md); the loose ends are in [open.md](open.md). This page
is the "what it actually is" reference.

> The exact measurements are not re-typed here — they live in one place the 3D
> room reads from, so they can never drift. Where a number matters for
> understanding, it's given in metres below, but the code is the source of truth.

## Shape and orientation

A rectangular room. The front-wall and back-wall are the long pair (about 8.83 m);
the entrance-wall and window-wall are the other pair (about 8.78 m). The ceiling we
work under sits about 3.52 m up — the real structural ceiling is higher (about
4.2 m), but the space above the beams isn't part of the design.

Think of standing in the back corner where the back-wall meets the entrance-wall:
the front-wall is straight ahead, the window-wall is off to your side.

## The four walls

- **Front-wall** — the long blank wall. This is the **loofah wall**: a bamboo
  frame surfaced in loofah fibre, warmly lit from behind. A touch-and-sight
  feature. *(No fireflies.)*
- **Back-wall** — the "piano wall." Two existing swing doors set into it, a piano
  against it, a fire-extinguisher box at one end. The air-conditioning heads (the
  "aircon units") are on this wall. *(No fireflies.)*
- **Window-wall** — an interior glass partition wall, not an outdoor window.
  Walking along it from the front-wall corner toward the back-wall corner: about
  119 cm of plain wall with a small window cut into it, then the ~99 cm silver
  service door, then ~90 cm of plain wall, then the big ~570 cm glass partition
  running to the back-wall corner. The HVAC plenum box hangs in the upper corner
  near the front-wall end. *(No fireflies.)*
- **Entrance-wall** — the wall opposite the window-wall. The visitor entrance is
  here: a full-height opening between the concrete column and the front-wall. *(No
  fireflies.)*

## The column and partitions

- **Column** — existing concrete, part of the building, sitting just outside the
  room in the corridor by the entrance. Not part of the new build, immovable.
- **Pathway-partition** — a new built **storage cabinet** (L-shaped) inside the
  room, separating the entrance pathway from the forest. Carries fireflies.
- **Entrance-wall-partition** — a new built **storage cabinet** along the entrance
  side. Carries fireflies.
- Both partitions are storage cabinets, roughly half a metre thick.

## Floor

Interlocking foam-mat tiles (about 0.6 m each): dark, matte, textured, no
reflectivity. No marble. Visitors keep shoes on.

## Ceiling

Sculptural suspended forms, hung as real objects, carrying the firefly LEDs in
pure green. The exact shapes, counts, and heights are still being worked out
(see [open.md](open.md)).

## The pathway and the forest

- **Pathway** — the walking strip visitors follow in from the entrance. A dark,
  near-black corridor that is **bright at the entrance** (gallery spotlights, so
  people can read information on the partition there) and **darkens as they walk**,
  until total darkness at the forest. Floor-level track lights and always-on warm
  edge strips run along it, with three matte-white painted arrows at the turns. No
  wood panelling. The window-wall side always keeps its blackout curtain (the real
  glass partition is there).
- **Forest** — the main body of the room beyond the pathway-partition, under the
  firefly ceiling. This is where the experience happens.

## Doors, windows, fixtures (existing, being covered)

Two staff doors on the back-wall, the silver service door and small window on the
window-wall, the HVAC plenum, the aircon heads. Their exact positions are still
estimated and need on-site measuring. The covering principle is: hide or
transform existing infrastructure, never feature it. Door openings are never
blocked by covering.

## What carries fireflies (recap)

Two places: the **ceiling forms** and the **partition cabinets**. The entrance-wall,
window-wall, and back-wall do not carry fireflies. The front-wall (loofah) is a
live maybe — see [open.md](open.md).
