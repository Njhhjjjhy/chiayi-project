# Chiayi indigenous Tsou people project

**Studio Minofmeer**

## 1. Timelines

The creative endeavors that are part of the timeline for the next few years include short term and long term projects, workshops, and various stages of research, production, and exhibition. The short term projects are expected to be completed within one to two projects by the end of 2023, while the long term projects involve a more intense focus with one to three projects per year. The workshops are scheduled to take place once every quarter, providing an opportunity for learning and development. The research phase involves collecting information, interviewing locals, and ideation and prototyping, taking up to one to two months. The production stage includes construction, creating objects, and collaborating with local artists, requiring two to three months. Finally, the exhibition stage involves showcasing the work, entering competitions, and applying for grants, taking up one to three months. Overall, this comprehensive approach aims to maximize creativity and innovation while engaging with local communities and showcasing the fruits of the labor to a wider audience.

## 2. Goals of the initiative

The Tsou community creative projects initiative aims to promote and preserve local crafts, culture, and aesthetics. The initiative has three main goals: craft, design, and education.

The craft goal seeks to create awareness and interest in traditional crafts of the Tsou people and integrate their own traditional works and skills into new pieces. The design goal aims to develop a unique Tsou design language that reflects the community's culture, values, and aesthetics, using resources such as architecture, stories, visuals, and symbolism. The initiative hopes to apply the developed design language to create new and meaningful projects and products in areas such as visual, branding, product, and furniture design. Finally, the education goal focuses on creating a learning environment for the local youth and visitors by developing workshops using knowledge from local craftsmen and outside designers and artists. The ultimate goal of the initiative is to promote and preserve the rich cultural heritage of the Tsou community while creating opportunities for sustainable economic growth.

## 3. Project infrastructure

The project infrastructure comprises three key elements.

First, an exhibition hall will showcase high-value finished works and works in progress that exemplify craft design and local craftsmanship. The aim is to foster a sense of pride within the local community and provide a space for tourists and the public to appreciate the pieces on display.

Second, the Tashan Tree of Light, an exhibition space for conceptual and experimental works by designers and artists, will aim to tell stories about the village and its people, with a particular focus on representing oral traditions and tales.

Finally, the project will establish workshops and education centers where designers and artists will teach local skills and introduce new techniques in different languages. These workshops will be held quarterly, providing a physical space for preserving and advancing local techniques and materials. Together, these three elements will create a robust infrastructure that fosters creativity, celebrates local culture, and provides opportunities for learning and growth.

## 4. Tools and resources

To successfully implement this project, several tools and resources are required. Machines such as woodworking, ceramics, and stone cutting equipment, as well as basic tools and machinery, are needed for creating craft, design, and art pieces and holding workshops. Complex machinery and tools can be self-built and assembled on site.

Expertise is also necessary from local craftspeople, designers, and artists from both inside and outside the village, and even from international sources. These experts would lead projects, create pieces for the various exhibition venues, and hold workshops to pass on their knowledge. The workforce, which would build the infrastructure, will comprise local villagers and volunteers from outside the village.

Raw materials such as lumber, bamboo, rattan, stone, wild clay, and various grasses for weaving will be sourced locally, while others like luffa, clay, and 3C products will be sourced from outside the region. By utilizing these tools and resources, the project will create a thriving creative environment that can showcase local craftsmanship and promote cultural exchange.

## 5. Details on possible projects

There are three potential projects, each with a specific focus, estimated time frame, and intended outcome.

The first project involves researching local oral traditions and folktales and converting them into a narrative-based design language. This would require two to three months of work and would result in a catalog of stories and imagery that could be used for branding campaigns, visual designs, and product and craft designs.

The second project centers around creating sculptural lighting pieces and installations using locally sourced bamboo and wild grasses, as well as imported materials like luffa and ceramics. The outcome of this experimental project would be to display the works at the Tashan Tree of Light.

Lastly, a series of exhibition pieces would be crafted using locally sourced wood, bamboo, and rattan. These pieces would incorporate traditional weaving and joinery techniques into contemporary furniture and lighting pieces in the style of Studio Minofmeer. The resulting series would showcase the Tsou region's resources and craftsmanship and would be displayed at the exhibition center, other galleries, and entered into competitions.
